# BRAND_GUIDELINES.md — Imberion

## Brand Overview
**Brand name:** Imberion  
**Tagline:** from noise to signal

Imberion transforms fragmented commercial information into structured intelligence and better decisions.

The brand should feel:
- strategic
- premium
- calm
- editorial
- precise
- intelligent
- restrained

The brand should not feel:
- loud
- trendy
- generic SaaS
- overly futuristic
- startup-template-like
- corporate in a dull way

---

## Brand Essence
Imberion helps organizations move from fragmented information and weak signals to structured insight and decision clarity.

Core conceptual movement:
**Noise → Signal → Decision → Value**

---

## Logo
### Primary logo
Use the full Imberion wordmark whenever possible.

### Secondary usage
Use the symbol or simplified mark only when space is limited, such as:
- favicon
- small UI contexts
- social avatar
- compact brand placement

### Logo rules
- Do not stretch or distort the logo
- Do not change the logo colors arbitrarily
- Do not add effects, shadows, outlines, or gradients to the logo
- Do not place the logo on visually noisy backgrounds without enough contrast
- Do not recreate the logo with another typeface

---

## Logo Variations
Preferred variations:
- full logo on light background
- full logo on dark background
- monochrome version when needed
- symbol-only version for small-scale use

Use the version with the highest contrast for the background.

---

## Clearspace
Maintain generous clearspace around the logo.

Minimum clearspace rule:
- use the height of the “I” or a comparable internal logo unit as minimum surrounding space

Never crowd the logo with:
- buttons
- navigation items
- borders
- imagery
- other marks

---

## Minimum Size
The logo must remain clearly legible.

Avoid using the full logo at very small sizes.  
Use the symbol-only version when the full wordmark becomes too small to read comfortably.

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors
**Deep Navy**  
- Role: primary brand anchor  
- Suggested HEX: `#1F3347`

**Off White / Warm Light Neutral**  
- Role: primary light background  
- Suggested HEX: `#F2F2F0`

### Secondary Colors
**Muted Blue Grey**  
- Role: secondary text, subtle support  
- Suggested HEX: `#8A97A3`

**Forest Green**  
- Role: restrained accent, signal color  
- Suggested HEX: `#214D36`

### Optional Supporting Tones
Use only if needed and only as derived tones from the palette:
- lighter navy tints
- lighter neutral backgrounds
- subtle border greys derived from navy

### Color Usage Principles
- Navy is the main anchor
- Light neutrals create space and calm
- Green is an accent, not the dominant color
- Grey supports hierarchy and legibility
- Avoid adding new unrelated colors

### Avoid
- default Tailwind blue or indigo as a primary
- loud gradients
- highly saturated accents
- rainbow palettes
- unnecessary decorative color usage

---

## Typography

### Primary Display Typeface
Use a serif display face for major headings and brand moments.

The serif should feel:
- elegant
- controlled
- editorial
- premium

### Secondary Typeface
Use a clean sans-serif for:
- body copy
- UI labels
- navigation
- buttons
- captions

The sans-serif should feel:
- modern
- readable
- quiet
- functional

### Typography Principles
- Strong contrast between heading and body styles
- Headings should feel deliberate, not heavy
- Body text should be highly legible and calm
- Use italics selectively for emphasis if it supports the brand
- Do not overuse bold

### Avoid
- overly dense text blocks
- tiny body copy
- generic startup type hierarchy
- decorative font mixing
- multiple competing display fonts

---

## Visual Style
The visual style should feel:
- editorial rather than templated
- premium rather than flashy
- structured rather than crowded
- calm rather than empty
- modern but not trendy

### Composition Principles
- strong use of whitespace
- clear hierarchy
- restrained use of accent color
- occasional asymmetry when it improves hierarchy
- fewer elements, better chosen

### Preferred Visual Signals
- typography-led sections
- elegant contrast
- clear spacing rhythm
- subtle motion
- high legibility
- intentional restraint

### Avoid
- cluttered dashboards as decoration
- excessive pills, badges, and feature boxes
- fake complexity
- generic AI visual clichés
- random glowing effects
- overly futuristic motifs

---

## Imagery Direction
Use imagery sparingly and intentionally.

Preferred imagery directions:
- abstract systems
- structured information
- data becoming clarity
- refined texture or pattern
- selective commercial / operational imagery when relevant

Avoid:
- robot imagery
- glowing brains
- stock-photo “AI people”
- generic dashboards with no meaning
- flashy, gimmicky tech visuals

---

## Iconography
If icons are used, they should be:
- simple
- quiet
- minimal
- geometrically consistent
- secondary to typography

Avoid:
- emoji-like icons
- random icon sets
- overly playful icons
- heavy icon dependence

---

## Buttons and UI Tone
Buttons and interface controls should feel:
- clear
- sharp
- premium
- restrained

Avoid:
- bubbly startup buttons
- excessive shadows
- oversized radii
- loud hover effects

---

## Tone of Voice
The brand voice should be:
- intelligent
- direct
- concise
- assured
- strategic

The copy should not feel:
- inflated
- hype-driven
- vague
- overly abstract
- full of buzzwords

### Prefer
- concrete outcomes
- clear language
- client-facing framing
- short, strong statements

### Avoid
- “AI-powered synergy” style language
- empty innovation language
- vague consulting jargon
- startup cliché messaging

---

## Brand Do / Don’t

### Do
- keep it clear
- keep it premium
- use whitespace generously
- let typography carry the brand
- make the business understandable quickly
- use the palette consistently

### Don’t
- make it look like a generic AI startup
- make it look like a generic SaaS template
- add noise for the sake of style
- over-explain with visual clutter
- use random colors or fonts
- confuse abstraction with sophistication
