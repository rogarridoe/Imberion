# CONTENT_GUIDE.md — Imberion

## Purpose
This file defines the content and messaging guidance for the Imberion website.

The website must communicate clearly and quickly.  
Follow the spirit of Steve Krug's *Don't Make Me Think*:
- make it obvious
- reduce cognitive load
- design for scanning
- avoid making the user decode the business

---

## Core Messaging Goal
A first-time visitor should understand within a few seconds:
1. what Imberion is
2. what problem it solves
3. who it is for
4. what action to take next

If the copy makes the user work too hard, simplify it.

---

## Messaging Priorities
Imberion is not just “AI”.

Imberion should be framed as:
- strategic commercial intelligence
- better structure for fragmented information
- clearer decisions
- commercial impact
- a system for turning noise into signal

Avoid sounding like:
- a generic AI startup
- a prompt wrapper
- a vague consulting firm
- a tool with no clear business impact

---

## Tone of Voice
The tone should be:
- direct
- intelligent
- controlled
- concise
- strategic
- assured

The tone should not be:
- inflated
- overpromotional
- buzzword-heavy
- vague
- trendy
- needlessly abstract

---

## Writing Rules
### Prefer
- short, strong statements
- concrete language
- business outcomes
- client-facing phrasing
- scannable structure
- clear verbs

### Avoid
- vague abstractions
- overlong paragraphs
- jargon without explanation
- empty AI hype
- language that sounds auto-generated

---

## Headline Rules
Headlines must be understandable on first scan.

### Good headline qualities
- explicit
- high-signal
- short
- outcome-oriented
- easy to parse quickly

### Avoid headlines that are only
- poetic
- abstract
- conceptual
- internally meaningful but externally unclear

Taglines can support positioning, but should not carry the whole meaning alone.

---

## Homepage Content Priorities
The homepage should answer these questions in this general order:

### 1. What is this?
The hero should explain what Imberion does in plain language.

### 2. Why does it matter?
Show the problem or gap clearly.

### 3. How does it work?
Explain the operating logic at a high level.

### 4. Who is it for?
Make the audience legible.

### 5. Why trust it?
Show credibility, operating depth, or strategic seriousness.

### 6. What do I do next?
CTA should be obvious and concrete.

---

## Hero Guidance
The hero must not feel abstract.

### Hero should contain
- one clear headline
- short supporting subhead
- clear CTA

### Avoid
- jargon-heavy framing
- too many moving parts
- multiple competing ideas
- internal framework language without translation

---

## Framework Language Guidance
Internal operating models are allowed internally, but must be translated externally.

Bad:
- Information Infrastructure
- Intelligence Layer
- Execution Layer

Better:
- We structure your data
- We generate decision-ready insights
- We help you execute and capture value

Always prefer client-facing language.

---

## Scannability Rules
Users scan before they read.

So:
- keep paragraphs short
- use meaningful section headings
- avoid dense blocks
- break information into digestible pieces
- keep hierarchy obvious

---

## CTA Rules
CTAs should be:
- direct
- concrete
- low-friction
- easy to understand

Prefer:
- Request a demo
- See how it works
- Talk to us
- Explore the approach

Avoid vague CTAs that do not tell the user what happens next.

---

## Credibility
Credibility should feel earned, not performative.

Use:
- clear business logic
- relevant operational depth
- serious framing
- confidence without hype

Avoid:
- fake-sounding claims
- random numbers with no meaning
- bloated proof sections
- exaggerated innovation language

---

## Content Do / Don’t

### Do
- make the business legible quickly
- use plain but premium language
- keep ideas concrete
- frame value clearly
- write for scanning

### Don’t
- sound like generic AI marketing
- over-explain
- hide meaning behind abstraction
- use empty buzzwords
- make users decode the business model
